How the Bitcoin Standard Leaderboard Works

What is Living on the Bitcoin Standard? Each person on this leaderboard has a logged start date for when they began living on the Bitcoin Standard — a lifestyle where you spend 70% of your monthly income on living expenses (spread evenly throughout the month) and save the remaining 30% in Bitcoin.

How is performance calculated? We simulate each person's journey from their start date to today. Every month, they receive income, spend 70% on living expenses distributed evenly across 30 days, and invest the remaining 30% into Bitcoin at that day's price. Their total Bitcoin holdings are then valued at today's price to show their accumulated purchasing power.

Key insights:

  • Time in the game matters — Early adopters like Max Keiser (2011) have been stacking sats through multiple market cycles
  • Consistency beats timing — Dollar-cost averaging 30% of income smooths out volatility
  • The 70/30 rule — Living below your means while building Bitcoin savings creates financial resilience
  • Compound growth — Bitcoin's long-term appreciation rewards patient accumulators

Note: Rankings are based on publicly known adoption dates. We assume a standardized $5,000/month income for comparison purposes. Actual holdings and income levels vary. The leaderboard updates live with current Bitcoin price data.
