Day 3 of the Kraken Card

March 4, 2026

Day 3 on the Bitcoin standard was a delicious one! Today, I grabbed both lunch and a celebratory cake at work, all paid for with Bitcoin. It's incredible how seamlessly these micro-transactions are becoming part of my daily routine, with a total spend of around 27,000 sats.

Beyond My Personal Spending

I noticed some interesting movements in the broader crypto space today. Krak, a platform many in the Bitcoin community are familiar with, just announced a 1% cashback incentive for users who get their salaries paid directly into Krak. This is a fascinating development and clearly shows they're setting their sights on competing with services like Strike and potentially disrupting the traditional bill payment landscape.

The Current Limitation

However, the current functionality presents a bit of a hurdle. While 1% cashback sounds appealing, the reality is that I can't yet pay my regular bills directly from Krak. This means that to leverage the cashback, I'd have to deposit my salary into Krak, then withdraw it back into my main bank account to cover all my standing orders and other expenses. For now, this extra step makes the 1% a bit questionable in terms of its real-world value.

Looking Forward

Despite this current limitation, I love seeing this kind of innovation! It's a clear signal that the financial world is waking up to the potential of Bitcoin. I'm eagerly looking forward to the day when Krak, or a similar service, integrates a robust bill payment feature. That's when these cashback incentives will truly become a game-changer, making "living on Bitcoin" even more practical and rewarding for the average person.

Why Now?

With Bitcoin prices at what feel like a turning point, starting to pick up momentum after a period of lower values, there's never been a better time to consider embracing the Bitcoin standard. The infrastructure is evolving rapidly, and every day brings us closer to a truly decentralized financial future.
