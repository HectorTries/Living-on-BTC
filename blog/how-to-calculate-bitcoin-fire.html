Step 1: Calculate Your Annual Expenses

Start by figuring out how much you spend each year. Include:

  • Housing (rent/mortgage)
  • Utilities and bills
  • Food and groceries
  • Transport
  • Healthcare
  • Entertainment and lifestyle

Step 2: Determine Your Withdrawal Rate

The standard FIRE withdrawal rate is 4% per year. This means you need 25x your annual expenses invested.

FIRE Number = Annual Expenses × 25

Step 3: Factor in Bitcoin's Growth

Bitcoin has historically appreciated significantly. When calculating, consider:

  • Conservative estimate: 30% annual growth
  • Moderate estimate: 50% annual growth
  • Aggressive estimate: 100% annual growth

Step 4: Calculate Your Target Bitcoin Amount

Example Calculation

Let's say your annual expenses are £30,000.

Traditional FIRE: £30,000 × 25 = £750,000

But with Bitcoin's growth, you might need less because your holdings can grow faster.

Key Variables That Affect Your Number

1. Current Savings

The more Bitcoin you already have, the less you need to accumulate.

2. Monthly Contribution

Regular DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging) accelerates your path to FIRE.

3. Time Horizon

More time = more compounding = less you need to save today.

4. Risk Tolerance

Higher withdrawal rates mean you need less but have more volatility risk.

Pro Tips for Faster Bitcoin FIRE

  • Stack sats daily — Buy small amounts consistently
  • Increase income — Use side hustles to buy more Bitcoin
  • Reduce expenses — Lower your FIRE number
  • Hold long-term — Avoid taxable events
  • Self-custody — Don't pay exchange fees
