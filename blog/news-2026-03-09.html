Here's what's happening in the Bitcoin world today — including some UK news!

🇬🇧 UK: Nigel Farage invests in Stack BTC

Nigel Farage has taken a 6% stake in Stack BTC, the Bitcoin treasury firm chaired by former Chancellor Kwasi Kwarteng. The Reform UK leader invested £215,000 in the equity fundraise.

This is a big deal for Bitcoin in the UK. You've got a former Chancellor of the Exchequer and the leader of a major political party both backing Bitcoin as a treasury asset. That's the kind of mainstream credibility that makes living on the Bitcoin standard feel less like a fringe hobby and more like the future.

🇩🇪 Germany: Zero tax on Bitcoin held over 1 year

Germany has introduced a 0% capital gains tax on Bitcoin held for more than one year. For short-term gains under a year, it's up to 45%.

This is a huge policy shift. It effectively treats Bitcoin like gold — a long-term store of value rather than a speculative asset. For hodlers in Germany, that's a massive incentive to keep stacking sats.

🇺🇸 US: Strike gets New York BitLicense

Strike has won its BitLicense in New York, clearing the path for its bitcoin financial services rollout in the US.
