March 2, 2026

As someone who's actually trying to live on the Bitcoin standard, I needed to find the best card. I've been testing the Kraken card myself. But I wanted to do a proper comparison with the other options on the market. Here's what I found.

The Contenders

For UK users looking to spend Bitcoin, there are three main options:

  • Kraken (Krak) - The new kid on the block
  • Revolut - The established fintech
  • Coinbase - The crypto giant

Kraken (Krak) - Best for Bitcoiners

Kraken's card is relatively new but it's designed specifically for people who want to live on Bitcoin. Here's what makes it stand out:

  • Real UK bank account - You get an IBAN with your name on it. This is huge. You can actually receive your salary directly into the account.
  • 1% cashback - Earn 1% Bitcoin cashback on everything you spend. No caps, no complicated tiers.
  • Zero fees - No monthly fees, no transaction fees, no FX fees. Literally zero.
  • Salary deposits - Your employer can pay directly into your Kraken account.

The catch: The spread is around 2%. But with 1% cashback, your net cost is around 1%. At current Bitcoin prices, that's actually not bad.

My experience: I've been using it for two days. The UI is slightly clunky but functional. The physical card arrives in a few weeks. The big win is having a real bank account with my name on it - something Strike doesn't offer in the UK yet.

Revolut - The Established Option

Revolut has been around longer and their app is much smoother. But for Bitcoiners, there are some issues:

  • Wide spread - If you buy Bitcoin through the main app, the spread can be 2-3%. That's painful.
  • Higher fees - Trading fees range from 0.49% to 1.49% depending on your tier.
  • Good app experience - Honestly, the app is much nicer than Kraken's.
  • More features - They offer stocks, commodities, crypto all in one place.

The catch: Unless you're on their top-tier plan, you're paying more in fees than you'd pay with Kraken. The convenience is nice but the costs add up.

Coinbase - The Big Name

Coinbase is the biggest crypto exchange in the world. But for UK users wanting to spend Bitcoin, it's not great right now:

  • No cashback in UK - Unlike US users, UK customers don't get any cashback rewards.
  • Higher fees - Trading fees are 0.4-0.6%, higher than Kraken.
  • No bank account - You can't receive salary or GBP deposits directly.
  • Great for buying - If you just want to buy Bitcoin, Coinbase is fine. But for spending, there are better options.

The catch: Coinbase is great for buying crypto but not great for spending it. Without a bank account option or cashback, it's not competitive for someone trying to live on Bitcoin.

The Verdict

For anyone serious about living on the Bitcoin standard in the UK, Kraken (Krak) is the clear winner. Here's why:

  1. Real UK bank account - You can receive your salary directly.
  2. 1% cashback - That's better than most traditional cards.
  3. Zero fees - No monthly, no transaction, no FX fees.
  4. Competitive trading fees - 0-0.26% is as good as it gets.

Revolut is fine if you just want to dip your toes in. But if you're actually trying to move your financial life to Bitcoin, Kraken is the way to go.
