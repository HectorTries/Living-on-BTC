Understanding the Labour Hour Analysis

Hours to Buy 1 Bitcoin (Min Wage): Shows how many hours someone earning minimum wage would need to work to afford 1 Bitcoin. This metric reveals Bitcoin's accessibility to the lowest-paid workers.

Hours to Buy 1 Bitcoin (Median Wage): Shows how many hours someone earning the median UK wage would need to work to afford 1 Bitcoin. This represents the typical UK worker's Bitcoin accessibility.

Work Years: Assuming a 36.6-hour work week (UK average), how many years of full-time work it would take to buy 1 Bitcoin at minimum vs median wage levels.

Key Insight: This analysis shows how Bitcoin's price evolution affects workers across different income levels and how accessible Bitcoin remains for ordinary people over time. The gap between minimum and median wage accessibility reveals the importance of income inequality in Bitcoin adoption.
